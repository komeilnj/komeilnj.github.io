Selected work

Propagation loss measurement and analysis

I worked on a free space optical bench for fabricated waveguides at 1550 nm. The project combines alignment, thermal control, photodetector readout, fringe contrast analysis, and a graphical interface for calculating the propagation loss coefficient.

The method is linked to low finesse Fabry Pérot resonances. Instead of relying only on absolute optical power, it uses the contrast of transmission fringes to estimate a combined loss and reflection factor, then derives an upper limit for waveguide attenuation.

Methods

How I approach a project

01

Define the measurable quantity

Translate the physical question into a signal such as fringe contrast, resonance shift, coupling efficiency, or image derived roughness.

02

Build the experimental or numerical model

Develop the optical setup, COMSOL geometry, Python workflow, or effective Hamiltonian needed to represent the system.

03

Connect data to the parameter

Use fitting, image processing, numerical sweeps, or machine learning to convert raw measurements into a physical result.

04

Test realism and limitations

Account for loss, noise, drift, thermal effects, fabrication tolerance, and the practical readout path.
